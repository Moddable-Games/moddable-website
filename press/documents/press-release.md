## Moddable.Games Launches Open-Source Tabletop Platform with First Title "Nukes" in Open Alpha

*UK startup brings modding culture to board games — rules are open-source, the engine is free, and anyone can create*

**Kuala Lumpur / London — May 2026**

Moddable.Games, a tabletop gaming startup founded in 2024, today announces its open-source platform for creating, remixing, and playing board games online — alongside its first original title, **Nukes**, now playable in open alpha.

### The Platform

Moddable.Games is a hex-grid online engine that hosts multiple tabletop game families from a single codebase. All game rules are published under CC-BY-SA 4.0 (free to fork, remix, and sell commercially), while the engine code is MIT-licensed. The platform currently runs:

- **Nukes** — a deterministic hex-tile strategy game for 2–3 players (nukes.moddable.games)
- **Moddable Chess** — a modular chess engine with 20 playable variants (chess.moddable.games)
- **Dungeon Chess** — an asymmetric skirmish game with four factions (dungeon.moddable.games)
- **Card Deck Builder** — a tool for designing and playing custom card decks

### The Game — Nukes

Nukes is a deterministic strategy game played on hex tiles. No dice, no luck — every outcome is calculable. Players deploy 6 tokens on a 44-tile modular board and compete through area control, chain reactions, and positional play. Games last 45+ minutes.

The game is designed to run on **standard Catan hex tiles** — meaning 45 million existing Catan owners can play with components they already have at home.

- **Components:** 44 hex tiles, 75 tokens
- **Rulebook:** v0.9.3 (open-source, CC-BY-SA 4.0)
- **Online:** Live alpha at nukes.moddable.games
- **Physical:** First print run targeting Q3 2026

### The Company

Moddable.Games was founded in Kuala Lumpur in 2024 and incorporated in the UK in 2025. The team of four spans design, engineering, art, and operations. The company is pre-seed stage, bootstrapped, and raising $250K–$500K through a mixed equity and royalty model.

### Licensing Model

The company uses a three-layer open-source approach:
- **Rules & mechanics:** CC-BY-SA 4.0 — anyone can fork, remix, and sell commercially
- **Code:** MIT — maximum contribution friendliness
- **Art & brand:** All Rights Reserved
